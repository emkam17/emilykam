What is Language Learning Tools?

As a Milstein Scholar, I have been working with other Milstein students who are interested in language, education, and coding to build an online learning tool for adult immigrants living in Ithaca. This tool will help them learn English more effectively and give them a chance to practice their English skills online with their peers.

This site is currently a work in progress.


The Problem

Since the pandemic, students at Open Doors English have had to communicate with teachers online through Zoom and email.

  • Current online method of communication and teaching is cumbersome
  • Students are not very comfortable with technology.
  • Existing sites like Canvas and Blackboard are too complicated for target users
  • Need to accomodate various levels of English understanding.

The Solution

After visiting Open Doors English to see how they operate and talking extensively with the teachers, my Milstein friends and I decided to code a site that would allow Open Doors English students to access all their assignments at once.


The Process

We want to make the interface as simple as possible, since our users will not be the most tech-savvy.

Using Figma, we started out with some simple sketches, which included a login page, a classes page, and a discussion board.

After our initial drafts and discussions/drawings for how to make the site user-friendly, we fleshed out our ideas, further simplifying the login and navigation and incorporating Open Doors English's color scheme from their website. We took inspiration from Google's Material Design.




Currently the site has a dicussion board for the teachers and other students to interact and a composition space for students to read and submit assignments. On their profile page, they are able to view their past posts and composition submissions.

To build our site, we are teaching ourselves JS, Vue and Firebase.
